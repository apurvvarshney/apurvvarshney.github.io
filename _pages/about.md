    <h1>Helping blind and low-vision people navigate the world.</h1>
    <p>PhD Student in the Bionic Vision Lab at UC&nbsp;Santa Barbara.</p>
  </div>

My work sits where computer vision meets human-computer interaction. In the [Bionic Vision Lab](https://bionicvisionlab.org/) at UCSB, advised by Prof. [Michael Beyeler](https://cs.ucsb.edu/people/faculty/michael-beyeler), I use mixed reality both to *simulate* what bionic-eye users see and to design real navigation aids for people who are blind or have low vision. You can see the projects I'm part of [here](https://bionicvisionlab.org/people/varshney_apurv/).

Before UCSB I earned my B.Tech in Computer Science &amp; Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology, Goa, where I worked with Prof. [Clint George](https://clintpgeorge.github.io/) on detecting breast cancer subtypes from human gene-expression data for my bachelor's thesis.

Away from research I shoot [photography](/photography/), ride bikes, and follow Formula 1 — though not all at once. For a fuller snapshot, here's my [résumé](/files/Resume.pdf).
